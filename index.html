Zeeve Rogoszinski

I am a sixth year PhD candidate at the University of Maryland at College Park, and I study orbital dynamics and planetary formation with Prof. Douglas Hamilton. I plan to defend my thesis in the summer of 2020.

For more information, please check out the links below or look at my CV.

Publications

Research

My primary focus is the origin of the spin-states of planetary bodies either through giant impacts or a spin-orbit resonance. I am particularly fascinated by Uranus, and I use numerical tools to help explain why it is tilted on its side.
